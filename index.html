Inter-Subject Variability in EEG-Based Stress Detection: A Machine Learning Perspective

"Can classical ML models generalize EEG stress patterns across unseen subjects, and what explains the performance ceiling under subject-independent validation?"

Problem Statement

EEG-based stress detection has demonstrated high classification performance under controlled conditions, but reliable cross-subject generalization remains largely problematic. Subject-dependent validation inflates accuracy by allowing models to memorize individual brain patterns. This work systematically evaluates classical ML under both SD and SI paradigms to quantify the generalization ceiling of feature-engineered approaches on the SAM-40 dataset.

Literature Review

Al-Shargie et al. (2017): EEG-fNIRS fusion for stress. Subhani et al. (2017): Ensemble classifiers for multi-level stress. Li et al. (2021): Dilated FCN for EEG emotion. Pandey et al. (2025): Graph-Transformer for cognitive workload. Nearly all SAM-40 benchmarks report subject-dependent results only (98-99%+), concealing the cross-subject generalization problem.

Research Gap

The generalization ceiling of classical ML under subject-independent validation has never been measured on SAM-40. Without this ceiling, one cannot determine whether incremental improvements are worthwhile, or whether the field must shift entirely to representation learning paradigms.

System Methodology

1

Dataset

SAM-40: 40 subjects, 14 channels (Emotiv EPOC+), 128 Hz, 4 cognitive tasks, 480 EEG segments

2

Preprocessing

Raw EEG, standardized pipeline: Savitzky-Golay filtering, Daubechies-2 wavelet thresholding

3

Features

Mean, Variance, Energy, RMS, Entropy, Kurtosis, Skewness, MAD, Peak-to-Peak, Std (top 10 = ~80% importance)

4

Evaluation

7 baselines + 3 ensembles. Dual validation: SD (StratifiedKFold) + SI (GroupKFold). Statistical tests.

7 Baseline Models

KNN (k=5), Random Forest (100), XGBoost, CatBoost, AdaBoost, Linear SVC, Logistic Regression

3 Ensemble Methods

Soft Voting (probabilistic), Bagging (bootstrap sampling), Stacking (meta-learner: Logistic Regression)

Statistical Validation

McNemar (Bonferroni), Friedman test, 10K Bootstrap CIs, Q-statistic diversity, Cohen's d effect sizes

Outcome

Achieved

Research paper submitted to international conference (Springer LNCS). Complete ML pipeline: 7 baselines + 3 ensemble methods, 3 binary tasks, dual validation, comprehensive statistical analysis including McNemar, Friedman, Bootstrap CIs, Q-statistics.

Future Work

Deep representation learning (1D-CNN, LSTM, Transformers) to address feature-space limitations. Hybrid calibration: subject-independent baselines with fast subject-specific adaptation. Cross-dataset validation on DEAP/DREAMER.
